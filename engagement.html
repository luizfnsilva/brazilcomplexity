Fit Assessment

Brief Categories

Four types of analysis

Each brief is scoped individually within one of four categories. Most engagements fall into a single category; some span two when the situation requires it.

Market Entry Brief

Analysis of entry mechanics for a specific sector, asset class, or business model in Brazil — entity structure, sequencing, professional stack, and execution path.

Regulatory & Municipal Complexity Brief

How federal, state, and municipal regulations apply to a specific situation, including licensing, permitting, agency interaction, and the gap between formal text and execution reality.

Real Asset Execution Brief

Asset-level analysis covering acquisition path, conversion or development feasibility, operator structuring, contractual frameworks, and yield expectations under local conditions.

Operator & Partner Risk Brief

Diligence framework for evaluating a Brazilian operator, partner, or counterparty — alignment risk, capability assessment, and structural protections to embed in the relationship.

Deliverable

What you receive

A written brief specific to your situation — not a template, not a market report. A document built around the actual question you are trying to answer.

Regulatory layer analysis

Which federal, state, and municipal regulations apply to your situation — and how they interact in practice, not just in text.

Execution reality assessment

How the relevant systems actually operate: approval timelines, enforcement patterns, failure modes, and what the formal framework omits.

Structural considerations

Entity structure, tax regime, contractual frameworks, and holding arrangements relevant to your specific asset class or entry mode.

Risk and gap identification

What the analysis does not resolve, where professional referral is warranted, and what questions remain open after the brief.

Confidentiality

How information is handled

No sensitive information is shared at the inquiry stage. The initial submission asks only for a general description of your situation — enough to evaluate fit, not enough to expose anything material.

If the engagement proceeds, both parties sign a mutual NDA before any confidential information is exchanged. The scope document, terms, and NDA are provided by email before any commitment is made on either side.

Client identity and engagement details are never disclosed. Engagements are accepted selectively and do not run simultaneously with competing interests in the same deal or market.

Process

How it works

Four steps. No calls required at any stage.

1

Submit an inquiry

Describe your situation briefly — what you are evaluating, what the complexity is, and what question you need answered. No sensitive information at this stage. Three to five sentences is sufficient.

Response within 48 hours.

2

Scope definition

If your situation is a fit, you receive a written scope by email: what the brief will cover, what it will not, the format of the deliverable, and the timeline. This is the moment to ask questions and adjust before any commitment.

No obligation at this stage.

3

Mutual NDA and confirmation

Once scope is agreed, both parties sign a mutual non-disclosure agreement. You then share the specific information needed for the analysis. An invoice is issued and payment is made before work begins.

NDA template provided. Straightforward, one page.

4

Delivery

The brief is delivered as a written document within the agreed timeline — typically 10 to 14 business days from confirmation. Format is PDF. A follow-up exchange by email is included if clarifications arise from the document.

Written. Asynchronous. Permanent reference.

Engagements are accepted selectively — typically one to two per month. If your situation is not a fit, or current capacity does not allow for it, you will be told directly within 48 hours. If there is a more appropriate resource or referral for your situation, that will be noted in the response.
